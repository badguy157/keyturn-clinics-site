Before/after: How we turned a dental clinic's "brochure site" into a booking machine

A real case study showing the patient-flow rebuild process, conversion lift metrics, and what actually changed in the first 60 days.

This dental clinic had a perfectly fine website: professional design, decent content, solid Google rankings. But "fine" wasn't moving the needle on new patient bookings.

The problem: Visitors weren't converting

The site got about 800 monthly visitors, but only 6 to 8 form fills or calls. That's a conversion rate under 1%. The issues:

  • No clear booking CTA above the fold
  • Generic messaging that didn't differentiate them from competitors
  • Slow mobile experience (3.2s load time)
  • Contact form hidden under "Contact Us" in the navigation
  • No social proof or patient testimonials visible on key pages

What we changed

1. Patient-flow focused homepage

We rebuilt the homepage with a clear hierarchy: headline clarifying their specialty (cosmetic dentistry and implants) → trust signals (reviews, years in practice) → prominent "Book a Consult" CTA above the fold.

2. Simplified navigation and CTAs

Reduced navigation items from 9 to 5. Made "Book Now" the primary CTA in the header, repeated it strategically throughout each page.

3. Mobile-first rebuild

Optimized images, removed unnecessary scripts, improved Core Web Vitals. Mobile load time dropped to 1.4s.

4. Trust signals everywhere

Added patient reviews, before/after photos, and staff credentials to the homepage and service pages.

The results (first 60 days)

  • Conversion rate: Up from 0.8% to 2.7%
  • Form submissions: Increased from 6 to 8 per month to 18 to 22 per month
  • Click-to-call tracking: Added tracking showing 12 to 15 additional phone calls per month directly from the site
  • Mobile conversion rate: Jumped from 0.4% to 2.1%

Bottom line: With the same traffic levels, the clinic went from about 8 inquiries per month to about 30 to 35. That's a 3.5x increase in leads from existing marketing spend, no additional ad budget required.
