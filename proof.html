CASE STUDY

The Anchor Hotel — Haydon Bridge

A family-run hotel in England was paying too much in fees to sites like Booking.com and Expedia. They needed more direct bookings. In 3–4 weeks, we fixed their homepage, made the booking buttons clear, improved the mobile experience, and set up tracking.

The same ideas (clear buttons, simple booking steps, and tracking) work for clinics filling appointment calendars.

What Was Wrong Before

The hotel's website had buttons all over the place, no clear path to book, and no way to see which visitors clicked to book. It looked OK but wasn't built to get bookings.

Clinics have the same problems: confusing options, unclear next steps, and no data on what brings patients.

Our Goals

  • Get more direct bookings and pay fewer fees
  • Add clear "Book direct" buttons on every page
  • Set up tracking to see who clicks to book
  • Improve the mobile experience and speed

What We Changed

  • Homepage: Clear hero with one main button. Less clutter.
  • Booking buttons: Added sticky booking bar and "Book Direct" buttons on all pages.
  • Booking path: Simple steps from any page to the booking system.
  • Tracking: Set up GA4 events to see who clicks to book.
  • Speed: Faster images, better mobile experience.

What Happened After

  • Search clicks +107% (85 vs 41) after launch
  • Click rate doubled: 5.1% vs 2.3%
  • Direct booking share: 18.8% (was 7.65%)
  • Projected annual direct share: 21.7%

Off-peak snapshot: 18.8%. Annual weighting uses seasonal data.

The Money Saved

Each booking moved off third-party sites saves ~$24.60 ($90.65 rate × 1.51 nights × 18% fee).

What This Means for Clinics

The same ideas (clear buttons, simple steps, good tracking) work for clinics. Instead of "more direct bookings," you get more booked appointments and higher-value patients.

A Simple Example

Say your clinic does cosmetic work or dental implants. One new patient for a high-value treatment might be worth $3,000–$8,000.

If a better website brings you 2–3 extra high-value patients per month, that's $6,000–$24,000 in extra monthly revenue. Even at the low end, the project pays for itself in under a year.

No fake math. This is a simple example. Your results depend on traffic, how many visitors book, and your treatment mix. But the pattern is the same: small improvements can mean real money when each patient is worth a lot.
