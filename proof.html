What Was Wrong Before

The Anchor Hotel's website had scattered CTAs, no clear booking flow, and no way to track which visitors actually clicked through to book. It looked fine but wasn't optimized for conversion.

Common issues clinics face too: multiple action paths confusing visitors, unclear next steps, and no data on what's actually driving consultations.

Our Goals

  • Lift website-direct booking share and reduce OTA commission drag
  • Create a unified, consistent "Book direct" CTA experience across all pages
  • Add proper GA4 event tracking to measure booking-intent clicks
  • Improve mobile experience and page load speed

What We Changed

  • Homepage structure: Clear hero with single primary CTA, removed clutter
  • Unified CTAs: Added persistent sticky booking bar + consistent "Book Direct" buttons across all pages
  • Booking flow: Streamlined path from any page to booking system
  • Tracking: Implemented GA4 events (booking_outbound & book_click) to measure intent
  • Performance: Optimized images, improved mobile experience, added structured data

What Happened After

  • Search clicks +107% (85 vs 41) in the period after launch
  • Search CTR improved: 5.1% vs 2.3%; average position 15.7 vs 23.7
  • Website-direct share: 18.8% in off-peak snapshot (baseline was 7.65%)
  • Projected annual direct share: 21.7% (seasonally weighted)

Off-peak snapshot: 18.8% (3/16). Annual weighting uses off-peak ≈9.8% of yearly bookings. In-season assumption: 22% website-direct.

What This Means for Clinics

The same principles—clear CTAs, streamlined flows, proper tracking—work for filling consultation calendars. Instead of "more direct bookings," you get more booked consults and higher-value patients.

A Simple Example

Let's say your clinic offers cosmetic procedures or dental implants. A single new patient for a high-value treatment might be worth $3,000–$8,000 in lifetime value.

If a better website and funnel brings you just 2–3 extra high-value cases per month, that's $6,000–$24,000 in additional monthly revenue. Even at the low end, the project pays for itself in under a year—and keeps paying dividends after that.

No fake math, no fabricated projections. This is a conservative, hypothetical example. Your actual results depend on traffic, conversion rates, treatment mix, and market dynamics. But the pattern is the same: small improvements in conversion can create meaningful financial impact when the transaction value is high.
